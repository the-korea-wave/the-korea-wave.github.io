South Korea's theatrical market cooled in the 27th week of 2026, with total weekly gross revenue down 2.7% from the prior week even as a fresh slate of four new releases entered the top ten, according to data from the Korean Film Council's integrated ticketing network (KOBIS). The decline came despite a stable top of the chart, signaling that softness was concentrated in the mid- and lower-ranked titles rather than the market leaders.

## A Softer Week Despite New Arrivals

The week saw four new entrants crack the chart, including *Marty Supreme*, the Doraemon feature *Nobita's Undersea Secret Fortress*, and *Greenland 2: Migration*. Yet the injection of new supply was not enough to lift overall takings: no title climbed in the rankings, while two titles slipped. The net effect was a market that leaned more heavily on its biggest draws rather than broadening out.

That concentration is visible in the numbers. The combined revenue share of the top three films rose from 70.6% to 74.0% week-on-week — an increase that typically points to a thinning middle of the chart, where holdovers lose momentum faster than newcomers can replace them.

## Toy Story 5 Leads a Two-Horse Race at the Top

*Toy Story 5* held first place with weekly revenue of about 6.07 billion won and 601,169 admissions, pushing its cumulative audience to 2,218,496. Close behind, *눈동자* took second with roughly 5.47 billion won and 537,472 admissions for the week, and a running total of 842,696 — a comparatively steep newer title still building its audience base.

The gap to the rest of the field was stark. Third-placed *군체* drew 128,292 weekly admissions for about 1.30 billion won, but its cumulative audience of 5,842,302 marks it as a long-running title deep into its theatrical life. Together, the top two accounted for the bulk of the week's gross, underscoring why overall revenue fell even as headline films performed.

## New Releases Land Modestly

Among the debuts, *Marty Supreme* posted the strongest opening in revenue terms at roughly 720 million won on 69,066 admissions, edging out fourth-placed holdover *Wild Thing* (665 million won, 69,575 admissions) on gross despite ranking one spot lower. The Doraemon feature opened in sixth with 39,156 admissions, and *Greenland 2: Migration* entered eighth with 35,155 admissions and about 335 million won.

Rounding out the ten, *Backrooms* (38,130 weekly admissions; 1,192,089 cumulative), *Supergirl* (26,699), and the *Assassination Classroom* feature (17,220) filled the lower ranks — none generating the scale needed to offset the broader decline.

## Takeaway

Week 27 fits a familiar pattern: a resilient top tier masking weakness beneath it. With top-three concentration climbing toward three-quarters of the market and every new release opening below the 70,000-admission mark, the 2.7% revenue dip reflects a market waiting for its next genuine breakout rather than one in structural retreat.
