Korea's weekly box office grew 4.7% in the twenty-eighth week of 2026, and almost all of that growth came from a single opening: the animated feature *Moana* entered directly at No. 1 with 511,463 admissions and 5.64 billion won in gross, more than a fifth of the entire top-ten haul on its first week in release.

The headline number, though, hides a narrower market underneath. Concentration in the top three titles climbed from 74.0% to 77.2% of weekly revenue, and not one film on the chart moved up a position. Seven fell. Two arrived from outside. In other words, the week's expansion was not a broad lift across the slate — it was one strong debut pulling the average up while everything already playing drifted down.

## One Opening Carried the Week

*Moana* took in 5,639,330,780 won against 511,463 admissions, all of it accumulated in the reporting week, which works out to roughly 11,000 won per ticket — the highest yield of any film in the top ten, consistent with premium and family-format screenings.

Behind it, *Eyes* (눈동자) held second place with 434,556 weekly admissions and 4.57 billion won, bringing its running total to 1,277,246. *Toy Story 5* placed third on 361,826 admissions and 3.79 billion won, with a cumulative audience of 2,580,314. Together those three titles generated just under 14 billion won, out of about 16.5 billion won across the full top ten.

## The Gap After Third Place Is Enormous

The drop-off below the podium is the most striking structural feature of the chart. Fourth-placed *Colony* (군체) drew 72,022 admissions — barely a fifth of the film directly above it — and No. 5, the new release *David* (다윗), managed 59,310 on an opening that reached a cumulative 75,469.

From there the chart thins out quickly: *Marty Supreme* at 31,828 admissions, *Wild Thing* at 29,618, *Doraemon the Movie: Nobita's New Undersea Secret Castle* at 17,093, *The Backrooms* at 16,980, and *Greenland 2: Migration* at 16,971. The bottom three titles are separated by roughly a hundred admissions each — statistically indistinguishable, and all of them under 200 million won for the week.

The practical consequence is that seven of the ten films on this chart are contributing marginal revenue to exhibitors. A cinema's economics in this week were set almost entirely by how many screens it allocated to three titles.

## A Fading Hit and a Fresh One, Side by Side

*Colony* offers a useful contrast in life-cycle position. Its cumulative audience of 5,914,319 is by far the largest on the chart — nearly twelve times *Moana*'s current total — yet it converted only 72,022 admissions this week. That is the profile of a film deep into its run, holding screens on reputation while its weekly contribution shrinks toward the noise floor.

*Moana*, at 511,463 cumulative, sits at the opposite end of the same curve. Whether the week's 4.7% gain proves durable depends largely on how steeply that opening figure declines, since the chart currently has no mid-tier title positioned to absorb the slack if it falls hard.

## Admissions, Not Revenue, Set the Order

One detail in the ranking is worth flagging for anyone reading these tables as a revenue chart. *Doraemon* placed eighth with 178,146,050 won, above *The Backrooms* at ninth with 191,941,600 won — the lower-grossing film ranked higher. The ordering follows admissions, and *Doraemon* sold 17,093 tickets to *The Backrooms*' 16,980.

The underlying reason is ticket price. The Doraemon release averaged roughly 10,400 won per admission, against roughly 11,300 won for *The Backrooms* — a spread of about 9%, reflecting the discounted child and family pricing typical of animated matinee programming versus standard adult evening rates. At these volumes, that pricing gap is enough to invert the two measures entirely.

## The Structural Read

A 4.7% gain reads as a healthy week in isolation. Read alongside zero upward rank movements and a three-point jump in top-three concentration, it looks more like a market that is increasingly dependent on the timing of individual wide releases rather than on sustained mid-tier demand.

The two new entries did their job: *Moana* delivered a genuine event opening, and *David* posted a functional if modest debut at fifth. But the seven incumbents all lost ground, and the chart's lower half is now operating at a scale where week-to-week rank changes carry little revenue meaning. The next meaningful test is whether *Moana*'s second week holds enough of its 511,463 opening to keep the total positive without another debut propping it up.
