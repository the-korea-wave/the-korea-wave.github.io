South Korea's theatrical market gained ground in the twenty-eighth week of 2026, with total ticket revenue rising 4.7% from the previous week. The lift came almost entirely from the top of the chart: two new releases opened directly into the upper ranks, no film below them climbed, and seven titles slipped, leaving a market that grew even as most of its individual films cooled.

## A New Leader Sets the Pace

The animated release Moana took first place in its opening frame, drawing 511,465 admissions and ₩5.64 billion in revenue — a figure it holds entirely as debut business, since its cumulative attendance matches its weekly total. That opening was strong enough to unseat the prior holdovers and reset the competitive order at the front of the chart.

Close behind, the drama Nundongja (The Eye) placed second with ₩4.57 billion and 434,556 weekly admissions, pushing its running total past 1.27 million viewers. Toy Story 5 rounded out the podium, adding ₩3.79 billion and 361,826 admissions to reach a cumulative 2.58 million — the deepest audience base among the week's active releases.

## Revenue Concentrates at the Top

The defining feature of the week was concentration. The three best-selling films accounted for 77.2% of all ticket revenue, up from 74.0% a week earlier. In practice, the market's 4.7% overall gain was driven by demand pooling around a small cluster of titles rather than by broad strength across the chart.

That imbalance is visible immediately below the podium. Fourth-place Colony (Gunche) earned ₩772 million against 72,022 admissions, but its cumulative 5.91 million viewers marks it as a mature title well past its commercial peak. The week's second new entry, David, opened fifth with ₩601 million and 59,310 admissions — a modest bow that nonetheless outperformed every holdover beneath it.

## The Long Tail Cools

Lower down, the chart thins quickly. Marty Supreme (₩346 million, 31,828 admissions) and Wild Thing (₩263 million, 29,618 admissions) held the sixth and seventh spots, while the animated Doraemon: Nobita's Secret Undersea City, Backrooms, and Greenland 2: Migration each drew fewer than 18,000 weekly viewers. Backrooms, at ₩192 million and 16,980 admissions, has accumulated 1.21 million viewers over its run, but its weekly pull now sits near the bottom of the top ten.

With seven of the ten ranked films declining week-on-week and none advancing, the chart's overall growth rested on the arrival of fresh product rather than sustained momentum among incumbents.

## What the Numbers Point Toward

The week reads as a handoff rather than a broad expansion. Moana and David refreshed the top tier and lifted the total, but the widening revenue share of the leading three films signals a market increasingly dependent on new openings to offset fading holdovers. Whether that 4.7% gain holds will depend on how quickly the next slate of releases can replace revenue as the current front-runners begin their own descent — and on whether any mid-chart title can finally reverse the downward drift that defined the rest of the week.
