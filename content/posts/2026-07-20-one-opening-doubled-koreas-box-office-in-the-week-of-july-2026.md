Korea's theatrical market roughly doubled in a single week, and almost all of that came from one title. Weekly gross rose 112.5% against the previous week, driven by the opening of *Hope* (호프), which alone collected 23.58 billion won and drew 2,119,041 admissions — about two-thirds of everything sold across the top ten.

## The Arithmetic of a Single Opening

The ten highest-grossing releases combined for roughly 35.07 billion won and 3,216,817 admissions. Strip out *Hope* and the remaining nine titles account for about 11.5 billion won — a figure broadly consistent with the pre-surge baseline implied by the 112.5% jump. In other words, the week's growth was not a broad recovery in moviegoing. It was one film arriving.

*Hope*'s cumulative admissions stood at 2,129,721 against 2,119,041 in the week itself, meaning 99.5% of its entire run to date was booked in these seven days. Its implied average ticket price works out to roughly 11,100 won, in line with standard Korean multiplex pricing rather than any premium-format skew.

The second new entry, *Minions & Monsters*, took 3.86 billion won on 370,947 admissions, with a cumulative figure of 374,784 — likewise essentially all of it from its debut frame. Two openers, then, supplied close to 78% of the week's top-ten revenue between them.

## Concentration Tightened Sharply

The share of revenue held by the top three titles moved from 77.2% to 83.8%. That kind of shift is the mechanical consequence of a large opening rather than evidence of audience preference narrowing, but it matters commercially: when the top three absorb more than four-fifths of spending, screen allocation for everything below tends to compress within days, and mid-tier titles lose the showtimes they need to build word of mouth.

The rank movement data underscores how one-sided the week was. Zero titles climbed the chart. Eight fell. Every incumbent gave ground to the two new arrivals, which is the expected pattern when a market this concentrated absorbs a wide release.

## The Holdovers Split Into Two Groups

Below the openers, the chart divides cleanly.

The still-active group is led by *Moana*, third with 2.75 billion won and 260,641 weekly admissions against a 772,104 cumulative — a third of its total run arriving in this week alone, which suggests it is still in its productive phase despite the rank drop. *David* (다윗) sits sixth with 84,301 weekly admissions on a 159,770 cumulative, over half its lifetime total, marking it as an early-run title rather than a fading one. *Marty Supreme*, eighth on 14,993 admissions, has 117,083 cumulative and is running at low volume.

The exhausted group tells a different story. *Colony* (군체) placed seventh with just 26,808 weekly admissions — against a cumulative of 5,941,127. Less than half a percent of its audience arrived this week; this is the long tail of what has clearly been the market's biggest performer of the recent period. *Wild Thing* is in the same condition, tenth with 7,654 admissions on a 1,340,887 cumulative. *Toy Story 5*, fourth on 169,869 weekly admissions and 2,750,183 cumulative, sits between the two states: still selling in volume, but with only about 6% of its run booked this week.

*Pupil* (눈동자), fifth with 1.65 billion won and 153,977 admissions on a 1,431,223 cumulative, and the Doraemon feature, ninth on 8,586 admissions, round out the chart.

## What the Numbers Do and Don't Establish

The verified data supports a specific reading: this was an opening-driven week, not a demand-driven one. The 112.5% figure describes a supply event.

What it cannot establish is durability. *Hope*'s second-week hold — the ratio of its next frame to the 2,119,041 it just posted — is the number that determines whether the market sustains this level or reverts. Korean wide releases that front-load this heavily frequently shed a majority of their audience in week two, but nothing in the current data predicts where this title lands. Similarly, the 83.8% concentration figure is a snapshot at peak; it will loosen mechanically as the openers decay, regardless of what happens beneath them.

The genuinely open question is the middle of the chart. *Moana* and *David* both show holdover profiles with room left to run, yet both lost rank this week purely on the arithmetic of the new arrivals. Whether they retain enough screens to convert that remaining demand is the part of this market the revenue headline does not describe.
