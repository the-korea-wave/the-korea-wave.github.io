Pop music tightened its grip on South Korea's live performance market in the fourth week of July 2026, rising to 52% of the leading genre's share from 50% a week earlier. Of the 50 productions on the box-office chart, 26 were pop concerts — more than half the field, and enough to make popular music the single most concentrated category as touring acts crowded into the summer calendar.

## A chart split between two genres

The week's ranking effectively belonged to two categories. Pop music led with 26 charting productions, followed by musicals at 16. Everything else was marginal: five plays, two Western classical programs, and a single dance entry rounded out the 50-title list. That distribution left roughly 84% of the chart in the hands of just two genres, with the remaining eight slots divided among three.

The pop surge was also a story of turnover. Thirty productions entered the chart for the first time, against eight that climbed and eleven that slipped — a churn rate that points to short-run concert dates cycling on and off the ranking rather than long-settled hits holding their positions.

## Musicals keep the crown, and keep it in Seoul

For all of pop music's volume, the single highest-grossing production was a musical: *Beethoven*, staged in Seoul, held the No. 1 position. Musicals in fact filled six of the top ten slots — *Beethoven* at first, then *Dracula*, *Gwanghwamun Love Song*, *Billy Elliot*, *Dear Evan Hansen*, and *Those Days* — and every one of them ran in Seoul.

That concentration underlines a structural divide in the market. Musical theater remains anchored to the capital's long-running venues, where established titles can sustain ticket sales across extended engagements. The genre's staying power at the very top coexists with pop music's dominance by count, a reminder that chart share and chart height measure different things.

## K-pop tours redraw the regional map

Where musicals stayed in Seoul, the week's marquee pop concerts pulled the market outward. The three productions ranked second through fourth were all touring shows staged outside the capital: ENHYPEN's *WORLD TOUR: BLOOD SAGA* in Busan at No. 2, 2PM's *THE RETURN* in Incheon at No. 3, and BOYNEXTDOOR's *TOUR: KNOCK ON Vol.2*, again in Busan, at No. 4. All three were among the week's new entrants.

The pattern placed two of the top four productions in Busan and a third in Incheon, with the French producer FKJ's Goyang date in Gyeonggi Province rounding out the top ten at No. 10. Arena-scale tours, in other words, are where the market's geographic reach lives: while musicals concentrate revenue in Seoul, the biggest pop acts distribute high-demand dates across Busan, Incheon, and the wider metropolitan region.

## What the week signals for the summer season

The two-point gain in pop music's share is modest on its own, but it arrives alongside heavy new-entry volume and a clutch of high-ranking tour dates outside Seoul — the profile of a peak concert season taking hold. If the balance persists, the near-term question is whether musicals can defend the summit against a widening base of pop concerts, or whether a single blockbuster tour landing in Seoul reorders the top of the chart outright.
